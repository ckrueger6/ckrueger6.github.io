International Trends Concerning Freedom of Information in the Electronic Commons

Freedom of information in the electronic commons is an internationally contested topic. Different countries have very different policies: authoritarian China heavily restricts online information, while capitalism in the US has created hotly contested policies like Net Neutrality, which is meant to protect the internet as a space for all information. As countries’ legal relationship with each other and technology evolves, the question of information freedom on the internet must be answered in national or global policy. The articles on this site discuss the differences between different countries' perceptions on information rights and what causes them, and the ethical debates surrounding internet restriction. Discrepancies between different countries’ information freedom policies can be harmful. Are these discrepancies harmful enough to motivate regulation on a global scale? The current and future trends around freedom of information on the internet are important to the future of our increasingly online society. The information age is booming, but the legal and ethical practices around online information have yet to become stable.
